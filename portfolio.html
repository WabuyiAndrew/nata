Meet Our Team

Awesome people behind our charity activities

Lois Chepkurui Sabila

Gender and Advocacy Officer

Lois Chepkurui Sabila is a passionate Human Rights/Environmental defender driving change as the Gender and Advocacy Officer at Nature Talk Africa.

With a strong academic foundation and a commitment to social justice, I bring a wealth of expertise and enthusiasm to the forefront of our organization. I graduated with honors from Makerere University in 2021, earning a Bachelor's Degree in Social Science. Expanding my horizons, I obtained a Certificate in Administrative Law in 2023 and pursued a Diploma in Human Rights, set to be completed in 2024, both from the prestigious Law Development Center. My journey is marked not only by academic achievements but also by a dedication to continuous learning. I honed my leadership skills from online programmes, acquiring valuable insights into effective team collaboration and leadership dynamics. At Nature Talk Africa, I have immersed myself in the realms of advocacy, proposal writing, and fundraising, contributing significantly to our mission. As a team player with robust leadership skills, I foster collaboration and innovation within our organization. My voice resonates with the passion of advocating for women's rights, empowerment of girls, and uplifting the youth. I am driven by a vision of a just system where everyone's rights are respected, emphasizing equality and inclusivity. My passion extends to environmental issues, particularly focusing on the intersection of climate change and social justice. I am dedicated to understanding how climate change impacts women, youth, and the girl child, advocating for adaptation and mitigation strategies that leave no one behind. In every endeavor, I am committed to creating positive change and fostering a world where diversity is celebrated, and justice prevails.
